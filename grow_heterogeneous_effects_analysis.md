# Heterogeneous GROW Impact Analysis

Effects are overlap-weighted subgroup contrasts reported as JMTES - JES. Negative values indicate lower absence or behavior severity at JMTES within that subgroup.

## Key patterns

- GROW-associated reductions in unexcused absence appear across all major student groups examined.
- The largest unexcused-absence reductions are in Grade 5 (-5.22 percentage points), Grade 1 (-4.86), and male students (-4.48).
- Total absence reductions are also broad, with the largest effects in Grade 5 (-4.72), male students (-3.77), and Grade 1 (-3.56).
- School behavior severity reductions are stronger for male students (-1.23) than female students (-0.32), stronger for Black students (-1.08) than NonBlack students (-0.37), and strongest by grade in Grade 5 (-1.65), Grade 3 (-1.45), and Grade 4 (-1.29).
- Bus behavior severity reductions are smaller in magnitude but generally favorable across groups, especially Grade 5 (-0.48), Grade 3 (-0.39), and male students (-0.33).
- Grade 2 is the main exception for school behavior severity, with a positive estimate (+0.65), suggesting no favorable school-behavior severity pattern for that grade in this dataset.

## Interpretation

The heterogeneity analysis suggests that the favorable GROW pattern is not driven by only one demographic group. Most subgroups show lower unexcused absence, total absence, and bus behavior severity at JMTES. Behavior impacts vary more by grade and subgroup than attendance impacts, so the behavior findings should be described as more heterogeneous. These subgroup estimates are exploratory because subgroups are smaller than the full sample and the study remains an observational two-school comparison.
