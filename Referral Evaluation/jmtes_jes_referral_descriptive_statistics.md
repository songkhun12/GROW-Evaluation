## Discussion

Descriptively, JMTES students had lower school-based referral frequency than JES students. The mean total school referral frequency was 0.27 referrals per 170 enrolled days at JMTES compared with 0.69 at JES, a raw difference of -0.42 referrals per 170 days.

The school-level breakdown shows lower average referral frequency at JMTES for Level I, Level II, and Level III referrals. Level IV referrals were rare at both schools, with a small positive raw difference for JMTES because the mean was 0.015 at JMTES and 0.007 at JES.

Bus referral frequency was also lower at JMTES in the descriptive comparison. The mean total bus referral frequency was 0.03 referrals per 170 enrolled days at JMTES compared with 0.16 at JES, a difference of -0.13 referrals per 170 days.

For bus referral levels, JMTES had lower average Level II referral frequency than JES and very low Level I and Level III bus referral frequencies in both schools. These level-specific descriptive statistics are useful for identifying which types of referrals drive the overall bus referral difference.

These referral descriptive statistics are unadjusted and should not be interpreted as causal estimates. They summarize raw school differences before applying overlap weighting, Poisson regression, or covariate adjustment.
